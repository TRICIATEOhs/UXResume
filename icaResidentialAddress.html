01

ICA - Update Residential Address

As part of ICA’s ongoing efforts to digitalise their services, this new e-Service is available since 1 October 2020. All Singapore Residents (Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents) who need to report a change of residential address can do so online.

Summary

Methods Used: Clickable prototype and usability testing

No. of Participants: 15

Role: Lead the project as the designated UX designer

Before this e-Service was implemented, Singaporean residents who have moved to a new address has to perform the change of address in person at the ICA building, Neighbourhood Police Post (NPP) or Neighbourhood Police Centre (NPC). Residents have to also present their IC and documentary evidence of their new address so as to change the address indicated on their IC.

New workflow

Residents will submit a form indicating their new residential address
A mail containing a unique PIN will be mailed to the new address
Residents need the letter to verify the new address with the PIN
Residents will login to the ICA e-Service and enter the verification PIN
Residents will receive stickers for their IC's new address

New feature

• Anyone will be able to perform this transaction for someone else as long as they have their SingPass credentials. This feature is targeted at elderly residents who are non-tech savvy

• Residents are required to attach the sticker to the back of their IC by themselves. There will be a simple guide instructing them on how to go about doing it

Before the usability testing, four sets of tasks was prepared for evaluation

We recruited a total of 15 participants for the Usability Testing for the different age groups:

  • 5 participants from the age range 20-39
  • 5 participants from the age range 40-59
  • 5 participants from the age range 60-79

Due to Covid-19, all Usability Testing sessions were all conducted via UXArmy, an online web tool that records the session. As UXArmy doesn’t allow for facilitation of the session, we decided to make use of Google Hangout as our primary communication tool while participants share their screens with us and perform the tasks via UXArmy. With that, we were able to capture the session (both video and audio), listen to verbal cues, and inquire about participant behaviors.

The objectives of this Usability Testing is to find out if the participant is able to:

  • Submit a request to change their residential address
  • Perform any follow-up action after completing the change of address form submission
  • Know the stage he/she is current at
  • Understand the terms used
  • Successfully complete the tasks provided
Task 1: Change Your Residential Address

“As newly weds, you have recently moved into a new apartment with your spouse. Perform the action to change your residential address to the new place”

Task 2: Receive a New Verification PIN

“You have waited for 5 days after your application and you have not received the verification PIN as mentioned. Perform the action to get your verification PIN”

The aim of this task is to evaluate a new feature for requesting a new verification PIN. I needed to observe how participants would navigate through the process to accomplish this task.

Task 3: Complete Your Application Process

“You have successfully received a new mail after requesting for a new PIN. Perform the action to complete your application process.”

I aimed to ascertain whether participants understood how to use the verification PIN.

Task 4: Change Residential Address for Others

“After you’ve shifted out with your spouse, your elderly parents (who are not tech-savvy) decided to downgrade their flat to a smaller unit. Perform the action to change the residential address on their behalf.”

Since this is a newly introduced feature, I sought to evaluate participants' ability to complete the task and gather any questions or concerns they may have regarding the feature.

I evaluated the design's functionality and coherence through usability testing with 15 participants

The original prototype was designed by a former colleague who has since left the organization. I assumed responsibility for conducting Usability Testing and improved the design based on feedback gathered from participants.

01

e-Services listing page

Upon logging into the e-Service, users will arrive at this listing page where they can choose the transaction they wish to complete.

Findings

  • Participants were unsure of what to do upon reaching the listing page and remained stuck there for a while
  • Participants created a “mental checklist” of what they have already done as there are no indication of their next step (Okay, I’ve already done this… I’ve done that too… I think I need to do this now…)

02

Persona selection

When clicking on any transaction listed above, users will be directed to a "Persona Selection" page where they must specify whether they are completing the transaction for themselves, for others, or for themselves and their family members.

Findings

  • Participants were unsure of what to do upon reaching the listing page and remained stuck there for a while
  • Participants created a “mental checklist” of what they have already done as there are no indication of their next step (Okay, I’ve already done this… I’ve done that too… I think I need to do this now…)

03

Form submission

If participants choose "For Myself and Others," the "Particulars of Applications" section will provide them with the option to add any number of additional applicants (family members residing with the main applicant) using the "Add Applicant" button.

Findings

  • I've noticed that some participants overlooked adding the second applicant and attempted to submit the form
  • Therefore, participants suggested that displaying the empty fields for the second person's particulars upfront would simplify the process and make the system more foolproof

04

Request for new PIN

After submitting the form, residents must await mail delivery within 3-5 working days. Yet, there might be instances where residents do not receive their mail. Thus, we devised Task 2 to assess this scenario. Our goal was to observe the steps participants would take in such a situation.

Once participants click on “Verify PIN” on the listing page above, they will be directed to the following screen. Only on this page will they see the option to request a new verification PIN.

Findings

This task experienced the highest rate of failure.

  • Participants didn't think of clicking “Verify PIN” because doing so implies they already have the PIN to verify. Since participants didn't receive the PIN, clicking on that option didn't seem logical
  • As such, some participants were looking for some kind helpline or chatbot to ask directly

I reviewed recordings and documented participants' clicking behaviors and verbal cues

After reviewing the recorded clips of all 15 participants, I noted their clicking behaviors, verbal cues, and potential opportunities to enhance the e-Service's user-friendliness. This enabled me to identify similar behavioral patterns and areas for improvement that may have been overlooked.

Subsequently, I consolidated my findings into a presentation slide and shared them with the stakeholders.

The design was reassessed and enhanced as a result of the findings

I was tasked with conducting only one round of Usability Testing. While there wasn't a formal evaluation session to assess the success of my updated design, I personally reached out to a few close friends and family members to gather their feedback.

01

e-Services listing page

Changes made

  • Incorporated icons to offer visual cues and enhance the depth of information
  • Reduced users’ cognitive load in remembering the steps they have already taken and the steps needed to be taken through a step-by-step design. This will also allow users to easily and quickly understand the entire process of this e-Service
  • Highlighted the primary call-to-action button - "Start" - while dimming other actions

02

Persona selection

Changes made

  • Incorporated icons to offer visual cues
  • Addition of tooltip for “Others” to give users more context on the purpose of this feature

03

Request new PIN

Changes made

  • Implemented "Progressive Disclosure" by presenting the "Click here to request a new PIN" option when needed. This ensures users don't need to search for the feature as it's readily displayed upfront.
  • Changing the textual information to fit current progress when users have already performed the task (e.g. “Not Started” to “Submitted on 01/Jan/2020”)

Among the various age groups, I noted an interesting observation

Participants aged between their 20s and 40s actively seek information, whereas those in their 50s and 60s prefer information to be straightforward and obvious.

The younger participants were comfortable navigating the site, using back buttons to return to previous pages.

However, the older participants were hesitant to explore, fearing they might make mistakes or be unable to undo them. Therefore, my updated design aims to alleviate cognitive burden on users.
