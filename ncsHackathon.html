NCS Covid-19 One-Stop Crisis Managemenet App

A one-stop crisis management (for contagious diseases) mobile application for NCS Employees to perform necessary measures needed to be taken during COVID-19.

10 mins read
Back to my work

Summary

Methods Used: NCS DRIVE (Discover, Reframe, Ideate, Visualise, Evaluate) and Agile

Duration: 2 Weeks

No. of Team Members: 3

Role: Team Lead

The NCS Hackathon 2020 aimed to harness employees' creativity and problem-solving abilities to address challenges encountered by clients or the organization amidst or following the COVID-19 pandemic.

I've long desired to participate in a hackathon but faced obstacles due to the absence of team members willing to take on the challenge. Nevertheless, recognizing its potential value, I successfully enlisted a group of colleagues who shared my enthusiasm.

We brainstormed existing issues ripe for a hackathon-style solution

Initially, we needed to identify existing problems suitable for a hackathon-style resolution. While we entertained several ideas, our final decision was to address an issue directly affecting NCS employees.

Amidst the challenges posed by COVID-19, NCS is striving to swiftly distribute information to employees while also gathering as much pertinent information as possible. As employees ourselves, we sometimes find ourselves inundated with the volume of information that needs to be processed.

Here's what we're referring to:

  • Subscribing to three NCS WhatsApp groups to receive updates on COVID-19 regulations
  • Receiving numerous emails detailing the chain of command for contacting if feeling unwell, including Reporting Officers, Health Managers, Floor ICs, Higher Management, etc
  • Dealing with a flood of various forms, such as contact tracing forms, temperature-taking forms, travel declaration forms, and requests to return to the office forms, all in different formats!

Problem Statment

How can we enable NCS Staff to execute the required COVID-19 measures in a manner that is more efficient (retaining information and achieving the same outcome with less effort) and organized?

Solution

A comprehensive mobile application for crisis management (focused on contagious diseases) tailored for NCS Employees to facilitate the execution of necessary measures during the COVID-19 pandemic.

I recognize that while the solution may not be revolutionary, time is of the essence. This idea seems the most feasible for quick implementation, enabling employees to use it promptly once deployed. Therefore, the team opted to proceed with this approach.

Following ideation, we began paper prototyping and initiated our mood board

During the ideation phase, we engaged in creating user stories and crafting paper prototypes to conceptualize the application's appearance. Utilizing the user stories enabled us to prioritize the essential features crucial for making our solution valuable.

We additionally created a moodboard to select the color scheme for our mobile application. Our primary inspiration stemmed from the colors in NEXUS (displayed in the top left-hand corner of the image below). However, we found these colors to be too vibrant and cheerful, which did not align with the tone we wanted for our crisis management application.

We aimed to avoid a dull and uninspiring appearance for our application. Therefore, we toned down the colors, transforming them into pastel shades. This adjustment resulted in a softer aesthetic that remains visually appealing.

Our focus shifted towards materializing our prototype and conceptual idea

This involves creating comprehensive storylines and user journeys that effectively demonstrate the functionality and value of our solution. Additionally, we meticulously design corresponding screens, ensuring that each interface element aligns seamlessly with the user experience we envision. Through this process, we aim to bring our concept to life in a way that captivates and engages our audience, effectively conveying the innovation and potential of our solution.

Given that we were all working remotely, we maintained regular communication by scheduling team calls via Microsoft Teams after each agile phase. These calls provided opportunities to exhibit our progress, clarify any inquiries, and disseminate any new discoveries.

We opted to conduct usability testing with only four NCS employees

Because of time constraints and limited participant resources, we opted to conduct usability testing with only four NCS employees. We observed their interactions to identify any potential issues while completing the assigned tasks.

The video presented below offers a brief glimpse into our Usability Testing process. Participants were given access to our clickable prototype and tasked with specific user actions. We facilitated communication through Microsoft Teams to gather feedback and insights from the participants.

Since the tasks were straightforward and we integrated them to the best of our ability, we received overwhelmingly positive feedback from our participants, requiring minimal changes.

Our team clinched a prize in the "People’s Favorite" category

In summary, it was an incredibly rewarding experience that I thoroughly enjoyed. Despite time constraints, as we were all juggling our full-time projects during office hours, I'm grateful for the proactive nature of my teammates. Collaborating with them was both productive and enjoyable.

Registration process and accessing app services

Features:

  • Onboarding Screens: Introducing users to the app's features
  • NCS Email Login: Hassle-free signup process
  • My Profile: Displays the user's category, office location and list of reporting officers. Reduces users' cognitive load to remember who their reporting officers are
  • Frequently Asked Questions: Provides access to COVID-19 and process-related FAQs, with an option to submit queries for FAQ list updates by the admin
  • Relevant Forms: Streamlined access to forms by centralising them within the app, transforming them into structured forms for easier submission to reporting officers

Receiving tasks for easy action completion

Features:

  • Notifications: Addressing the Floor IC's need to track staff whereabouts, the app will send notifications to staff on-premises, prompting them to return to their office for a briefing
  • Pending Tasks: Reducing users' mental burden by introducing pending tasks required before they can leave for home
  • Mobile Usage: Transitioning from paper-based attendance marking and medical supply acknowledgment to a mobile application to minimize contact among colleagues during contagious disease situations
  • Easy Contact Tracing: Simplifying contact tracing by providing a list of recent NCS contacts or colleagues from the same department for easy addition to the system

Reporting fever symptoms

Features:

  • Notifications: Notifications will prompt staff to take their temperature twice daily. If they have a fever, they can easily report it to their officers
  • Simplified Reporting: This app provides a step-by-step guide for your next actions
  • Integration with HRCentral: Medical Certificates will auto-attach to HRCentral for easy submission without VPN or laptop access
