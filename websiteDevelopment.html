Summary

I did not have much hands-on experience with front-end development when I was a Java Web Developer. Some UX designers may argue that those skills are not so important for those who are mainly involved in prototyping and UI design.

However, I choose to believe that having the capabilities and knowledge of front-end development is essential in designing realistic solutions.

GetHRRight.sg

GetHRRight is a project requested by one of my relative who is soon thinking of retirement and would like to provide professional HR consultancy services to Organisations (i.e. through conducting of workshops, review HR policies).

This site has been through its revamp towards the end of 2019. The initial site design was developed in 2017 utilising a One-Page website structure. However, I found the design to be too old-fashioned and the layout did not cater for an expansion of content.

Due to unforeseen circumstances, we have taken down the website and it’s no longer in operation.

Church Website

I have been wanting to revamp my Church's website for quite a while now. This is just a wireframe that I did during Circuit Breaker period and when I have time on my hands, I will be developing it into an actual website.

The design of the website may not look the most exciting and unique, but rather “bootstrap themed” looking. The rationale for this is because visitors of this site are here to perform an action - such as downloading a weekly bulletin or getting updates on latest happenings.

The longer they linger on this website could potentially indicate that they are lost or could not find what they came looking for. Hence, I wanted to focus more on the Information Architecture rather than User Interface.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

I was also quite curious as to what other churches have on their website. Hence, I went to look for a list of churches in Singapore and went through every single website available.

I bookmarked a few churches and took some references from them on how they organised their information.
