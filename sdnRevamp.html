Social Development Network Redesign

Contrary to common belief, Government websites can indeed be aesthetically pleasing. There exist various strategies to redesign them while maintaining compliance with DSS standards. I present my approach to the Social Development Network (SDN).

10 mins read
Back to my work

Summary

The Social Development Network (SDN) operates under the Ministry of Social and Family Development (MSF), with its primary objective being to establish the groundwork for promoting healthy relationships among Singaporean singles by facilitating increased social interaction opportunities.

As a personal project, I have taken the initiative to redesign only the homepage of SDN, not the internal pages. It's important to note that I am not associated with this Government agency.

I wanted to know what are some common stigmas associated with SDN

Based on research findings and insights from conversations with my single friends, here are some stigmas associated with SDN:

  • Apart from the outdated appearance of the current site, singles perceive SDN as a government agency that promotes marriage, primarily targeting older individuals who have struggled to find a life partner
  • The younger generation views SDN as a platform for desperate singles who are introverted and not conventionally attractive. Despite its superficial nature, physical appearance heavily influences people's perceptions, as evidenced by psychological research
  • With the prevalence of free dating mobile apps, singles question the necessity of paying for SDN's dating events. Moreover, the lack of event reviews or trial opportunities makes it challenging for singles to gauge what to expect before committing to an event

Problem Statment

How might we effectively draw Singaporean singles of all ages to visit SDN, fostering greater awareness of its objectives, motivating participation in dating events, and encouraging exploration of the site?

Solution

While a robust marketing strategy is vital for attracting traffic to the site, refreshing the website with updated design and features is equally crucial to enhance visitors' experience. One prevalent design trend in 2020 is the use of vibrant colors to convey a sense of energy and youthfulness.

User Persona

I sketched wireframes and gathered visual inspiration to create a mood board

At this phase, I began by determining the content for the homepage and creating a sketched layout through wireframing. Subsequently, I sought inspiration by analysing different elements from various dating sites and apps. These insights guided me in developing a mood board, which served as a blueprint for designing the screens.

In 2020, vibrant and playful colors emerged as a prominent trend in design. Utilizing such colors not only aligns with current design trends but also suits the nature of a dating website. The aim is to impart a modern and refreshed look to the site, enhancing its appeal to users.

01

Above the fold

Incorporating a group of individuals in the hero banner imbues a sense of human connection, establishing a welcoming tone for the site.

The prominent call-to-action button underscores SDN's marketing strategy, offering free $100 credits to incentivize user engagement.

The gradient effect utilised is visually striking and memorable, adding a colorful and playful touch. Such design elements are uncommon in government sites, contributing to the site's distinctive appeal.

02

About SDN

Positioning an "About Us" section immediately after the fold is crucial for SDN, given its comparatively lower recognition compared to entities like the Ministry of Manpower (MOM) or Ministry of Health (MOH).

Many visitors to SDN will likely be newcomers, unfamiliar with the site's layout and navigation. They may need to explore the site akin to "Dora the Explorer" before engaging with the navigation menu.

Given SDN's focus on relationships and love, I incorporated a subtle heart-shaped backdrop to infuse a playful element into the design.

03

SDN Events

In consideration of Vanessa's preferences and the identified stigmas, it's evident that singles may be hesitant to pay for dating services or applications. Therefore, it's essential to include a call-to-action at this point, informing visitors that they are eligible for free $100 credits. This incentive encourages them to participate and experience events for free.

Think of your favorite mobile game: it allows you to start playing for free and gives you time to determine if you enjoy it. If you do, you might not mind spending some money to purchase additional "game gems" to enhance your gaming experience.

My strategy follows a similar principle. It's imperative to highlight affordable events from the outset, as featuring a $200 event, exceeding the value of the free credits, might discourage users at this early stage.

04

Dating Information

Lastly, SDN offers more than just events; it also provides engaging dating content to help individuals prepare for real-life interactions.

To enhance click-through rates for this content, incorporating numbers in titles can be highly effective. Studies have shown that readers tend to scan text rather than read every word. Numbers act as "Brain Candy," capturing the attention of readers. Particularly, odd-numbered digits are perceived as more authentic and appealing to our organizational minds.

"Well, it's still going to be awkward..."

Revamping the website isn't the sole method to drive traffic. It's essential to ensure that dating services offered by agencies address the needs and concerns of Singaporean singles, particularly in handling awkward silences.

While personalized matching services may pair individuals based on shared interests, the ability to maintain a conversation is equally critical for success.

Although further research and surveys are necessary, a friend suggested an intriguing concept: incorporating "facilitators" to assist during awkward moments, similar to a double date scenario. I found this idea quite innovative and promising.
