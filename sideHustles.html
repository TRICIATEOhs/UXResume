    Disclaimer

    The purpose of my "Side Hustles" is to sharpen my skills in front-end design & development, as well honing my skills in User Experience.

    I did not have much hands-on experience with front-end development when I was a Java Web Developer. Some UX designers may argue that those skills are not so important for those who are mainly involved in prototyping and UI design. However, I choose to believe that having the capabilities and knowledge of front-end development is essential in designing realistic solutions. All projects done are pro bono (unless stated otherwise).

    Most of my projects are Government-based and if you would like to know the details of my work, I will share more during the interview itself.

    GetHRRight is a project requested by one of my relative who is soon thinking of retirement and would like to provide professional HR consultancy services to Organisations (i.e. through conducting of workshops, review HR policies).

    UXResume

    https://triciateohs.github.io/UXResume/ [2020]

    My UX portfolio is also a project!

    • Research: More people are utilising online resumes and I thought that maybe it is time for me to hop on the bandwagon as well! I Googled ways to create a UX portfolio and what people would be interested to look at!
    • Design: I did some basic paper prototyping here and there, and whipped out Adobe XD to form the structure of my website. I researched a lot on pinterest as well as looking at other UX Designers' portfolios. Most of them were utilising third-party tools with existing templates to build their site, and yes it was definitely very eye-catching and interactive. I am more of a pastel/neutral-toned kind of person, and as much as I wanted to stand out, I decided to choose a colour scheme and do whatever my heart desired - well, I kind of have the flexibilty to do what I want, so why not? :D
    • Develop: Following the wireframes that I designed, I utilised bootstrap as a base for my development - and it's mobile friendly too!

    If you have any feedback on how I can further improve my online portfolio, do let me know!

    Application of Permanent Residency in Singapore

    When I was still a Web Developer, I was in-charge of maintaining and enhancing a system that allows public to upload documents into a repository when they are applying to become a Permanent Resident in Singapore. While utilising the system to perform testings, I felt that the user experience could be further improved. Hence, I've decided to take the initiative to improve the experience and make it more user-friendly.

    As the system had to undergo a technical refresh, it could use a new look-and-feel. Hence, I did my research and proposed my solution to the team for consideration.

    The goal of the project is to move towards a paperless approach, to reduce the need for public to come down physically to submit documents so as to improve the efficiency of e-Services application process.
