Lah-gage: Travel Itinerary Mobile Application

Have you ever wondered where your friends traveled during their holidays, or asked for tips on what to do in places they’ve explored? I have, and that’s why I designed this travel app that lets you upload, explore, and connect through personalized itineraries!

10 mins read
Back to my work

Summary

Whenever we had the chance to travel around the States, I often found myself asking friends where they were planning to go, where they had been, and what they would recommend. These conversations would turn into long discussions filled with stories, photos of must-see spots, and food recommendations. But more often than not, we’d forget most of it. And if they sent me a bunch of Google Map links, they’d get lost over time as well.

That’s when I started thinking it would be fun to have an app where you can easily showcase the places you visited and the experiences you had while traveling. Sure, it might not be the most groundbreaking product out there, but I wanted to create something simple and useful—an app where you can keep all your travel memories in one place, and inspire others with your journeys.

I put together a simple storyboard based on what I've experienced, and see if it resonates with you too!

Although my drawing is questionable, but there's no question that this has happened to us before!

Wait a minute, aren't there tons of similar travel applications out there?

I've downloaded more than five travel itinerary apps (i.e. Viator, Wanderlog, Stellar, TripIt, Tripsy) available in the market to explore for myself, but none of them matched my vision. While I don’t doubt they may work for some users, many of them either fall short of expectations or attempt to be too 'versatile and flexible.' For example, adding features like a bill splitter to a travel app makes it overly complicated in my opinion.

My vision for 'Lah-gage' is to create something incredibly simple and straightforward. Users can easily upload their itineraries and share them with the world, allowing others to see what they’ve been up to and incorporate those experiences into their own plans.

Let me introduce you to 'Lah-gage'!

The name 'Lah-gage' is a play on the word 'luggage,' with a local twist. I wanted to give it a bit of Singaporean flair, reflecting the way we pronounce it.

Homepage

The layout is designed to be intuitive and engaging, offering personalized suggestions while also encouraging users to share and explore.

  • Search bar: Find your next destination and explore itineraries from other creators
  • Ongoing trips: Plan collaboratively with friends or share your trip details at the end of the day
  • Explore more on 'Singapore': Get personalized recommendations based on your 'Ongoing Trip' or current location
  • Explore other countries: Discover new destinations through itineraries from other users

Create new trip

Creating a new trip is easy. Users can choose to 'change the cover' of their itinerary post, or a default stock image will be used. The itinerary details are kept simple, letting users focus on building their travel content.

Additionally, selecting trip mates allows friends to collaborate and edit the content together.

Create itinerary details

There are two main features for filling out itineraries.

  • Check-in: The 'Check-in' feature captures the user's location, date, and time for spontaneous activities, allowing users to add details later
  • Add item: The 'Add Item' feature allows users to organize their itinerary into categories such as activities, food, and lodging. Additionally, we've included a 'Location' category for unmarked or hidden spots—like a perfect wedding photoshoot location or a great spot to watch the sunset—that aren't listed on Google.

Wait, what happens if I plan to visit multiple cities?

When traveling to certain countries, you might find it beneficial to explore multiple cities—something that simply isn’t an option when you’re in Singapore. To enhance the user experience in these scenarios, we’ve included an additional tab that highlights the various cities you’ve visited based on the activities you’ve added to your itinerary.

This feature is especially helpful for public viewers, enabling them to navigate specific cities of interest without sifting through the entire itinerary. By clicking on this tab, users can quickly access information about different cities and their associated activities, allowing for a more focused exploration.

Exploration page

On the exploration page, the app features the top three popular trip destinations tailored to users' preferences or based on other travelers in the same country, eliminating the need for manual searches.

Recognizing that itineraries can cover multiple states, particularly for road trips, I've designed the display to show the starting and ending destinations (e.g., Osaka > ... > Tokyo) to accommodate space constraints.

Filter search

The filter search allows users to refine their search criteria. For instance, some users may be looking for a three to five day itinerary that covers Tokyo, Osaka and Nara. Rather than searching for individual activities, they can easily browse through other creators' itineraries and 'copy' those that match their needs.

View details of activities

On the detail page, users can scroll through images shared by creators, with their usernames and bookmark counts displayed. This allows users to explore full itineraries if they’re interested.

Given the effort creators put into capturing photos, we want to showcase their work by using these images as cover photos. These will be randomized and different for each user to feature a variety of itineraries. With the help of AI, we can ensure the cover photos focus on the actual destinations, avoiding selfies or unrelated content.

In travel, things don't always go as planned

I felt it was important to show whether users recommend a place or not. Just because something is on an itinerary doesn’t mean it’s worth visiting. Adding thumbs-up and thumbs-down icons can help other travelers decide if a spot is truly worth their time.

Add first, fill the details later!

In this example, once the creator has added 'Senso-ji' to the itinerary, it will be displayed as a blank entry. This allows the creator the flexibility to fill in the details at their own pace, whether that means adding photos, notes, or personal experiences about the activity.

View profile

Like all social media platforms, profile viewing is a crucial feature that enhances user interaction. On the left side, we display the profile view of a creator, where they can access itineraries that are still in 'draft' mode. This allows creators to manage and refine their itineraries before sharing them with the public.

On the right side, public viewers can see itineraries once they are published. At this stage, they have the option to bookmark their favorite itineraries for easy access later. This bookmarking feature encourages engagement, as users can save itineraries they find appealing, allowing them to revisit and plan their trips based on the curated experiences of others.

Overall, this profile view fosters a sense of community and collaboration, making it easier for users to share and discover travel plans.
